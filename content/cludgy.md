The brief was to take the dark courtyard and create a seating space with light for more of the day than the current yard offers, there are many other houses and trees around casting shadows that we realised we could mitigate by raising the seating area, a plan was formed to create a decking area above the replacement outhouse, the Cludgy being a name for a similar structure used by my Aunt.

Unfortunately I dont have any before pictures, but essentially a collapsed tin shack, following cleanup the foundations were shored and repaired and I built Aircrete block walls, I needed the strength to take the weight of the roof and the large deck above, these were then clad in local Larch sourced from the Lleyn Peninsula, where a lot of sustainable re-planting is done to our post war forrests.

The roof is a gentle slope with neoprene membrane for waterproofing, above and around sits the frame for the decking, a material from [Dura Composites](https://www.duracomposites.com/) The grating chosen for its lightweight yet strong construction with the ability to let light into the shadowed courtyard, a fiber reinforced composite capable of spanning larger gaps, the supports are timber 50 x 100 mm joists at minimum of 1m apart, and I can say with confidence that the material worked exceptionally well, still in excellent condition 13 years later. The grating has small holes that allow some light down through to the underside where there is still a coal bunker. I would also add although my memory is old now, dealing with Dura Composites was a dream, I spoke to a guy named Ben who facilitated a small offcut for me to use as the company deals in large comercial projects normally, I hope they still have this wonderfull support team today.
